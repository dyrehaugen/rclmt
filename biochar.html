Biochar uses pyrolysis and subsequent rapid carbonization of biomass to enrich carbon in a stable form for storage in soil. Production of biochar imitates the natural organic carbon pathway by accelerating the biogeochemical processes responsible for transferring a fraction of organic carbon from the biosphere into the geosphere.

Alternatively, rapid carbonization of biomass in oxygen-depleted natural wildfires could expedite the maceralization process. Production of biochar imitates the latter process, using controlled heating pyrolysis of biomass to rapidly carbonize and transform the organic matter into the inertinite maceral. The degree to which the carbonization process has achieved the complete transformation of biomass into the inertinite maceral is an important outcome that defines the biocharâs permanence.
